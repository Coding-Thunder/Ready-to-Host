PIVMaster



This is based on purely Indian technology with most advanced 3D Lagrangian Particle Imaging Velocimetry (PIV) method for the densely seeded flows at the highest resolution and at the highest speed simultaneously. Compared to all the other PIV systems worldwide, the system is undoubtedly the best of its kind where it does not just characterize the particle size in 3D, but it is boosted with very high speed imaging technology based on Indian patents. The patent publications are listed in separate main menu of the website. This Indian technology is now being used in many companies worldwide. This is simultaneously based on a purely iterative Particle/object Reconstruction (IPR) technique, in combination with a highly advanced alternative imaging platform, supporting algorithm that fills the response time / inactive time of the one imaging sensor with the active time / imaging time of another imaging sensor. It has the highest reconstruction accuracy at the fastest image grabbing rate and the fastest processing speed compared with all its global counterparts. Besides different software packages PIVMaster systems are using almost the same hardware for its different variants.



ATRPIV



The time resolved velocity measurements are widely accepted worldwide which are basically the information about particles in two frames of single camera. We deliver a unique system, Advanced Time Resolved PIV (ATRPIV) comprising of the same patent publication used in PIVMaster. This does not just improve the speed of the imaging but the resolution too. The measurement volume under the focus is of few millimeters size for the microscopic analysis.




APTVMaster



Well known, globally, as a particle tracking velocimetry, where, the particles set in the motion are imaged in a single reference imaging frame illuminated by multiple time resolved flashes. Yet the global system suffers from a major issue of reduction in the resolution with increase in imaging speed. This limits the imaging speed as the resolution itself goes down to the object size. We came up with the unique-to-the-world solution to build Advanced Particle Tracking Velocimetry, where the resolution is kept the same and the imaging speed is doubled through our technological advances. This increases the accuracy of the measurements as the motion blur of the object is removed or significantly reduced due to the high speed of the imaging system as compared to the global counterpart. This has enabled the motion tracks without any motion blur in a halved time. The halved time reduces the system requirements and specifications, further reducing the price of the systems. This saves the cost of the hardware.




IFOF PIV



This PIV is a technique based on more qualitative than quantitative mapping of the flow field to facilitate the physical insight into the domain of interest to characterize the overall fluid behavior. This is also called as Planer PIV as it extracts the measurement data and fluid structure visualization in a plane in contrast to the point measurement method. As the name indicates, this is In-Focus-Out-Of-Focus measurement volume characterization technique. The principle of operation can be understood from the diagram. A coherent laser light is focused by lens to create a measurement volume at its focal length. The grid in focus has several disks of 60 micrometers located equidistant. The profile plot of each disk is shown in the intensity plot. The intensity plot of disk in focus has highest amplitude value. The dimple at the peak is indicative of in-focus position of the grid. As the grid of disks is moved out of the focus point, intensity profiles show gradual diminishing characters. The imaging speed and distance between grid movements (in cm) is indicative of particle velocities and size. Relation between intensity profile plots locates the virtual planes at which particles are imaged. The grids are only used for the calibration and the particles injected in this region are characterized. Eulerian fluid is characterized using seeding particles.



Blur Master PIV


A motion blur is often seen as a disadvantageous and unwanted effect produced due to the inadequacy of fps speed of image grabbing systems. This is also the effect due to the use of the adequately equipped visualization system for the high velocity fields in flow domain. The discarding of blurred data is always considered and well-practiced as a solution in research and industrial sector to get rid of this data. We harvest the very disadvantageousness of the motion blur for the extraction of object velocities and size and propose and build Blur Master PIV.
